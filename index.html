Final Exam & Final Grades — Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Please read this page carefully before emailing your TA, Head TA Grant Seiter, or Professor Lee Coppock. Most questions we receive after the final exam are answered below.

Before You Email

Before sending an email, please make sure that you have:

  • Read the Final Grades announcement on Canvas in full
  • Reviewed the grading scale and examples provided there
  • Checked your final letter grade on SIS
  • Checked your scores on Canvas
  • Confirmed that your question is not answered below
Please note: Being close to a cutoff is not grounds for a grade change.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Where can I see my final letter grade?

Your final letter grade will be posted on SIS.

Canvas displays component scores (midterms, final, clicker points, TA points), but SIS is the official record of your final letter grade.

2. How was my final letter grade calculated?

Final grades are calculated using two methods (the “Dutch Knockout”), and you receive the higher of the two:

  1. Final-exam-only method
  2. Cumulative total method (midterms, final exam, clicker points, and TA points)

A detailed explanation — including examples — is provided in the Final Grades announcement on Canvas. Please review that announcement carefully.

3. Where can I find grade cutoffs and summary statistics?

All grade cutoffs, examples, and summary statistics for the final exam are posted in the Final Grades announcement on Canvas.

That information is not duplicated here.

4. Will there be any grade “bumps” or curve adjustments?

No.

There are no grade bumps, rounding, or discretionary adjustments, regardless of how close a student is to the next cutoff. The grading scale is final and will not be changed.

5. Can I see my final exam or review questions I missed?

The final exam is a testing exercise, not a learning exercise. Unlike the midterms, we do not review the final exam or go over individual questions.

If you believe your exam was graded incorrectly due to a clerical issue, contact your TA and clearly describe the specific issue.

6. I was very close to the next letter grade. Can my exam be reconsidered?

No.

Proximity to a cutoff is not a basis for re-evaluation. All students are held to the same grading standard.

7. What if I believe there was a clerical error?

Grades are reviewed only for clerical errors, such as:

  • A score entered incorrectly
  • An arithmetic error when summing scores
  • Clicker points, TA points, or another component not included due to a recording mistake

If you believe this applies to you:

  • Contact your TA as soon as possible
  • Clearly and concisely describe the specific clerical issue
  • Do not request a re-grade based on judgment calls or cutoff proximity
8. Should I email multiple instructors or TAs separately?

No.

Please contact your TA, and only if you believe there was a clerical error. Emailing multiple members of the instructional team separately will not change the outcome and may delay a response.

9. Can I appeal my final course grade?

No.

There is no appeal process for the final exam or for grading cutoffs.

10. Who should I contact if my question isn’t answered here?

If your question is not addressed in this FAQ and does not concern a clerical error, it is likely that no further action is required.

For clerical issues only, contact your TA. If needed, you may also contact Head TA Grant Seiter or Professor Lee Coppock.

Thank you for a great semester. Professor Coppock and the ECON 2020 instructional team wish you a restful break.
